Understanding Bitcoin Mining Efficiency

July 2026 · 10 min read

What is J/TH?

Joules per Terahash (J/TH) is the standard measure of Bitcoin mining efficiency. It tells you how much energy is required to produce one terahash of computing power. Lower J/TH means better efficiency — you get more hashing for every watt of electricity.

For context, a modern Antminer S21 operates at around 15-17 J/TH, while the BitAxe with its BM1397 chip typically achieves 50-80 J/TH. The BitAxe isn't designed to compete with industrial miners on raw efficiency, but its compact size, low noise, and open-source nature make it ideal for home mining.

The Efficiency Equation

Mining efficiency is calculated as:

Efficiency = Power (W) / Hashrate (TH/s)

A BitAxe consuming 15W at 1.2 TH/s has an efficiency of 12.5 W/TH (or 12.5 J/TH). If you overclock to 1.5 TH/s but power draw increases to 20W, efficiency drops to 13.3 J/TH — more hashrate but less efficient.

Factors Affecting Efficiency

1. Temperature

The BM1397 becomes less efficient as temperature increases. Every degree above 65°C adds measurable overhead. This is why proper cooling isn't just about preventing damage — it directly impacts your mining revenue.

2. Voltage and Frequency

Each chip has an optimal voltage/frequency curve. Running at higher voltage increases hashrate but disproportionately increases power draw. The sweet spot varies per chip — this is why factory-tuned miners test each unit individually.

3. Power Supply Quality

A PSU operating at 85% efficiency means 15% of your electricity is wasted as heat before it even reaches the miner. Using a high-quality 80+ Gold or Platinum PSU can save you 5-10% on electricity costs.

4. Ambient Temperature

For every 10°C increase in ambient temperature, the miner's cooling system works harder (fan speed increases, consuming more power). Mining in a cool basement is more efficient than a hot attic.

Measuring Efficiency Over Time

Efficiency isn't static — it changes with temperature, voltage stability, and chip aging. This is why long-term monitoring matters. HashWatch tracks efficiency trends so you can see:

  • Gradual efficiency decline that may indicate chip degradation
  • Efficiency improvements after cleaning dust or improving airflow
  • Impact of ambient temperature changes across seasons
  • Comparison of efficiency across multiple miners to identify underperforming units

Maximizing Your Efficiency

  • Cool the environment: Keep ambient temperature below 25°C. A $20 desk fan can improve efficiency by 5-10%.
  • Use a quality PSU: 80+ Gold or better. The efficiency gain pays for itself in months.
  • Don't overvolt: The default voltage is factory-tuned for a reason. Only experiment if you understand the risks.
  • Clean regularly: Dust buildup on heatsinks can reduce cooling efficiency by 20-30% over 6 months.
  • Monitor and adjust: Use HashWatch's efficiency trend charts to identify the optimal operating point for your specific hardware and environment.

The Economics of Efficiency

At $0.12/kWh, a BitAxe at 15W costs approximately $1.30/month to run. At 1.2 TH/s, the expected Bitcoin revenue is roughly $2-4/month (depending on difficulty and pool luck). This means efficiency directly impacts whether your miner is profitable.

A 10% improvement in efficiency (from 12.5 to 11.3 J/TH) saves about $0.13/month in electricity while maintaining the same hashrate. Over a year, that's $1.56 — small per miner, but significant if you run a fleet.
