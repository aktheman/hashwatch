Miner Monitoring Best Practices

July 2026 · 6 min read

Why Monitor Your Miners?

Bitcoin mining hardware runs 24/7 under heavy load. Without monitoring, a failing fan can lead to thermal damage, a WiFi dropout can mean hours of lost hashrate, and a pool issue can silently drain your profitability. Proper monitoring catches these issues early and keeps your miners profitable.

Key Metrics to Track

  • Hashrate — The most direct measure of mining performance. Track both instantaneous and rolling averages to spot gradual declines.
  • Temperature — The BM1397 should stay below 80°C. Consistent readings above 75°C indicate airflow problems.
  • Power draw — Monitor wattage to calculate efficiency (J/TH). Rising power at the same hashrate suggests hardware degradation.
  • Share acceptance rate — Above 99% is healthy. Below 95% indicates network or pool issues.
  • Uptime — Unexpected restarts are the first sign of power supply or WiFi problems.
  • Voltage stability — Fluctuations in voltage can indicate PSU issues that may damage the board.

Setting Up Effective Alerts

Not all alerts are equal. Here's a tiered approach:

Critical Alerts (act immediately)

  • Miner goes offline for more than 5 minutes
  • Temperature exceeds 85°C
  • Share rejection rate exceeds 15%

Warning Alerts (investigate soon)

  • Hashrate drops more than 20% from baseline
  • Temperature consistently above 75°C
  • Fan speed drops below normal range

Informational (check weekly)

  • Efficiency (J/TH) trending upward
  • Uptime below 95% over 7 days
  • Minor share rejection fluctuations

Building a Monitoring Routine

Even with automated alerts, a quick daily check takes 30 seconds and can prevent costly issues:

  • Morning: Glance at the dashboard — are all miners online? Any overnight alerts?
  • Weekly: Review the 7-day chart for each miner. Look for gradual hashrate or efficiency changes.
  • Monthly: Clean dust from heatsinks, check fan operation, verify pool statistics match your dashboard.

Organizing Multiple Miners

If you run more than one BitAxe, organization becomes important. HashWatch supports groups to help you manage:

  • By location: "Office", "Garage", "Living Room" — know at a glance where each miner is.
  • By model: Group BitAxe Ultra, Gamma, and Max separately for accurate comparisons.
  • By status: Create a "Needs Attention" group for miners flagged by anomaly detection.

Advanced: Using Anomaly Detection

HashWatch's AI anomaly detection goes beyond simple thresholds. It uses rolling averages and statistical analysis to detect:

  • Gradual hashrate decline (may indicate chip degradation)
  • Temperature spikes that don't correlate with ambient changes
  • Voltage fluctuations suggesting PSU problems
  • Uptime drops that indicate random restarts

These detections include confidence scores, so you can prioritize which miners to investigate first.
